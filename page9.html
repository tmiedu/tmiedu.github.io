SHORT COURSES & SEMINARS

The nature of dynamic times and how they affect businesses and challenge management requires organizations to constantly keep the human capacity on top of the game. This is because if it is not learnt and known it is not practised. Our response to this phenomenon, is to create hands-on learning opportunities for managers and leaders’ and for their immediate applications at workplaces. We develop relevant short courses on local and global topical issues that advance the course of better competitiveness, growth and development.

Our faculty deliver these courses through class presentations/discussions, demonstrations, simulations, role plays, teamwork, case studies, company visits and exercises including projects to bring learning in knowledge and skills to bear on the specific needs of participants.

Who should attend
Professionals, industry leaders, and managers at various levels in corporate hierarchy and persons interested in advancing corporate growth and development of their organizations, are invited to enroll in these courses

 MANUFACTURING/TECHNOLOGY

MT17011: Manufacturing capacity management.

MT17011:

The need to make manufactured products available and affordable have continued to challenge management of manufacturing businesses. They have also heightened the quest for better applicable technologies and processes. In these regards, researches, innovations and
breakthroughs continue to emerge, and in their successes, complicated management of manufacturing capacities. This course discusses modern solutions to these issues including the today’s revolutionary additive manufacturing/3-D Printing and technology and its impacts on capacity optimization.  

FOOD AND AGRIBUSINESS

FAM11021: FOOD PROCESSIONG AND QUALITY CONTROL MANAGEMENT

FAM11021:

The roles food play and good quality food for that matter is today assuming greater dimensions and importance, because of its impacts on health and ability of human resources to advance economies. Achieving good quality foods both in quantity and quality begins with processing and attention given to quality standards and control measures throughout the chain; from all input supplies end to all consumption
ends. What this course does is bring participants to speed on best practices on food processing and control management that keep organizations sustainably competitive.

FAM17022: FOOD VALUES
CHAIN MANAGEMENT 

FAM17022: 

Every demand internal or external to the food chain looks up to some value expectations. These value expectations are what facilitate exchanges between the supplier and the demanders in the supply chain. Competitively sustaining
growth and development requires understanding expected values in the chain and providing these values as demanded. This course fortifies participants with modern techniques for identifying and managing food value chain. 

FAM17023: GROWING AND DEVELOPING COMPETITIVE
AGRIBUSINESS. 

FAM17023:

The agribusiness industry is in the heart of economic advancement in our world then and now. Its growth and development is at the center of all growth, development and
industrialization of economies. The challenges of commensurate proportion in growth and development of the industry require greater attention today than ever. This course is designed to expose participants to building the right capacity to manage growth and development of agribusiness that meet today’s world needs.

HEALTHCARE ENTERPRISE MANAGEMENT

HEM17031: HEALTHCARE

HEM17031:

Healthcare services’ processes are by their peculiar nature dominated by hybrid procedures and activities. Achieving good healthcare delivery services therefore demands appropriate service-process design and management. HEM17032: LEADING AND MANAGING HEALTHCARE ORGANIZATION.

HEM17032: 

Healthcare organization is made up of the organization itself and all the resources it has including human, money, materials as well as its customers. Leading and managing all these to deliver right expectations in a particularly unique industry in any economy asks for special attention to knowledge, skills and expertise. TMI in this course, presents to participants, specially crafted experiential learning approach to good leadership and management of healthcare organization.

HEM17033: HEALTHCARE
DELIVERY AND TECHNOLOGY 

HEM17033:

Application of technology in healthcare delivery the world over and at various stages of societal development has been there and cannot be said to be new. What has become a special phenomenon today is the rate of technology advancement and complexity in the roles technology is playing in healthcare delivery. This course brings participant to date through unique learning procedures and processes in knowledge and best practices, about healthcare delivery and technology; impacts on improving accuracy of healthcare delivery including the revolutionary 3-D printing technology applications

CONSTRUCTION AND BUILDING

CB17041: CONTRACTING AND CONTRACT
MANAGEMENT 

CB17041:

Properly deciding on undertaking a project or providing works, goods and services to achieve objectives, is on one hand, on the other important hand is the challenge of engaging relevant service providers to realize the objectives. This involves contracting the services out to providers and managing the contract effectively and efficiently. Contacting and contract management is the focus of this course.

CB17042: COSTING AND PRIZING ENGINEERING
PROJECTS 

CB17042:

Under an experiential hand on learning delivery methods, this course takes participants through common pitfalls in project costing and prizing. As project is a time line based undertaking, prizing a project must firstly recognize all cost elements as an important input to prizing. This course discusses the techniques and processes of achieving efficient engineering project costing and prizing.

CM17043: PROJECT CRITICAL TIMELINE MANAGEMENT

CM17043:

Projects are embarked upon to deliver to beneficiaries in given time otherwise the values to be delivered with project are lost. What causes missing out on timely deliveries of projects is inadequate attention to critical timelines inherent in all projects.
The essence of this course is to equip participants with ability to identify and properly manage critical project timelines.

CB17044: WORK PLANNING AND IMPLEMENTATION

CB17044: 

Every human undertaking in construction and building industry particularly governed by objectives for measuring achievements, is a composition of various types of works. Realizing the objectives is dependent on proper planning and execution, through adequate allocations and utilization of required resources. The need for better planning and implementation of works in this industry is increasing by the day. What this course offers participants is global trends in knowledge, skills and best practices in works planning and implementation.

CB17045: UNDERSTANDING AND MANAGING COSTS DETERMINANTS OF
INFRASTRUCTURE PROJECTS 

CB17045:

Economies and organizations in the industry are determined to improve infrastructure base for growth, development and industrialization. Correspondingly, they are confronted with challenges of identifying and managing infrastructure projects costs determinants. This course is designed and delivered to offer participants in a hands-on learning experience manner that build the ability to identify, understand inherent complexity of the determinants and manage them adequately.

CB17046: ESTABLISHING
PROJECT BID PROCEDURES, PROCESSES AND DOCUMENTS 

CB17046:

For any project to be successfully delivered, multiple parties must be involved in delivering various services. These services have to be offered by right person or persons, at right quality/quantity, and at right costs within a given time frame. What is being demanded of all parties in this case is proper performance under fit- for-purpose conditions.
To select the right parties, opens up a major question, and that is what will be the governing conditions, procedures and processes? This course introduces and enhances the knowledge, skills and expertise of participants on how to establish and manage fit-for-purpose bid procedures, processes and documents to deliver projects.  

TRANSPORT AND LOGISTICS

TL17051: DISTRIBUTION AND LOGISTICS MANAGEMENT

TL17051:

All goods and services from sources of supply to utilization points in whatever form including electronic formats must be distributed through one form of transportation and logistics system. Today, the timeliness and efficiency of it all are at issue. What participants benefit form outcomes of this course is the ability to effectively and efficiently manage distribution and logistics of resources within and between internal and external suppliers and demanders.

TO17052: TRANSPORTATION AND INTERMODAL
INTEGRATION MANAGEMENT 

TO17052: 

In the course of economic and industrial activities, goods and services are moved from one place to another and usually involving multiple modes of transportation. The intermodal nature of such activities and attendant challenges of ineffectiveness and inefficiency require corresponding knowledge, skills and expertise for better results. This course responds to these and offers participants through well-articulated and delivered learning methods the ability to understand better and competitively manage transportation and intermodal integration.

OPERATIONS MANAGEMENT 

OM17061: PRODUCT DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT

OM17061:

What organizations exchange for values with customers are their products. This could be works, goods or services. The acceptance of the product by customers is paramount to continuous existence of organizations operations. Designing and developing product that is producible, and meets laws, regulations and customers’ preferences are the core impacts of this course.

OM17062: HIGH VELOCITY RESOURCES AND INVENTORY MANAGEMENT

OM17062: 

The costs of businesses operations, developing and industrializing economies have remained inversely proportional to rate of development and industrialization in favor of costs. The challenge today is to reverse this trend and achieve better positions of proportionality in favor of development and industrialization. This involves not allowing resources inventory to be idle, because idle resources exhibits zero velocity and wastefulness. This course raises required knowledge, skills and expertise of participants, through experiential learning to manage high velocity resources and inventory in their organizations for better performance.

OM17064: RESOURCES
PROCUREMENT MANAGEMENT. 

OM17064:

The process of acquisition of resources from supply end to consumption ends is one set of activities that habit tendencies of ineffectiveness and inefficiency. These are usually revealed in lack of capacity and capability or inappropriateness of purchaser, supply source, intermediary coordinator where applicable and governing conditions of the procurement. What this course is designed and delivered to achieve for participants is build ability to meet current practices in managing procurement.

OM17063: UNDERSTANDING PROJECT COSTING AND PRIZING

OM17063: 

Projects by their nature are not going concerns, they have time limits within which their delivered values remain useful. That inherent character in projects makes its costing and prizing complex with myriads of considerations, requiring special knowledge, skills and practices. This course prepares and enhances participants’ ability to be proficient in costing and prizing projects.

OM17064: BUSINESS
PROCESS DESIGN AND CAPACITY MANAGEMENT 

OM17064:

All organizational undertakings follow processes. A well decided process facilitates efficiency and effectiveness. Otherwise is wasteful. What this course help you do is to have the knowledge and skills to understand what informs choice of good process. It discusses factors for designing, developing and profitably managing business processes.

OM17065: SUPPLY CHAIN MANAGEMENT

OM17065:

The challenges today in taking goods and services from remotest supply sources to the remotest points of need at most effective and efficient manner is globally topical to industry leaders and mangers. The entire supply chain seeks special attention of industry players at all levels. This course is designed and delivered to participants to institute the right capacities and capabilities to be sustainable and competitive in managing goods and services supply chain.

OM17066: ELIMINATING COST ADDING DECISIONS AND ACTIVITIES.

OM17066:

Decision making is a key function of businesses and so is value adding activities; for purposes of organizations beneficially achieving set goals. This however depends on how valuable or cost adding such decisions and activities are. Industry leaders and managers are involved in quite a lot of decision exercises and activities, with attendant effects on corporate performances. The costs and contributions of many of these decisions are not easily noticeable, giving rise to unmanageable cost adding rather than value adding consequences. Value adding decisions/activities and their effects are preferred for profitability. Achieving these is to eliminate all cost adding decision and activities. This course provides experiential learning methods through which participants recognize and understand cost adding decisions/activities and be able to eliminate them in the course of managing their businesses.

OM17067: PRODUCT QUALITY MANAGEMENT

OM17067:

Quality generally is understood to mean what the consumer says it is. As simple as this explanation may seem, the challenge is that this acceptance various from one consumer to another and is dependent on regulations and standards, as well as place, when, how and what conditions, physical or biological the quality judgment is to be made.
The product designers, producers and regulators, also impacts on product quality judgments with challenges of differing preferences and acceptance. How then do businesses manage product quality to remain in business? This is the focus of this course; to strengthen participants’ ability to manage product quality in the midst of varying preferences.  

GENERAL MANAGEMENT

GM1707: CORPORATE
GOVERNANCE AND
TRANSITION MANAGEMENT 

GM1707:

This course raises capability of managers and executives to provide corporate governance. It discusses corporate goals setting, deciding strategies that work and managing organizational
transition.

GM17072: MANAGING
EMPLOYEES’ ENGAGEMENT, UTILIZATION, RETENTION AND EXIT 

GM17072:

If an employee is not performing as expected, it is most likely that the employee is a wrong hire, or that the organization has not done enough to encourage obtaining better employee’s output. This course offers participants the tools to get organization staffing right from engagement, through retention, utilization to exit management.

GM17073: LEADING
PEOPLE AND ORGANIZATION 

GM17073:

Organizations regularly set out to achieve certain predetermine goals. Setting these goals and defined ways of realizing them are factors of human participations. In an organization with people at work, what influences expected organizational performance has a lot to do with leadership. And because organizations must realize their targets and remain competitive, leadership must deliver on these. Our experienced faculty deliver this course to enable you upgrade your capability to lead your organization in competitive environment.

GM17074: BRINGING DOWN NON-PHYSICAL BARRIERS IN ORGANIZATIONS FOR BETTER PERFORMANCE

GM17074:

In workplaces, there are undeniably high tendencies and occurrence of build-up of barriers within and between organizational units and persons. These barriers are non-physical and in most of the cases not visible but severely experienced. They are drawbacks to organizational achievements and must be identified and eliminated. This course offers you the ability to understand sources of these barriers, their effects and how to manage their elimination in organizations.

GM17075: CORPORATE DECISIONS MAKING

GM17075:

In organizations, decisions must be made. And what is of greater interest is the correctness of such decisions. A wrong decision is expensive and brings down performance levels. This course presents you best ways and processes of undertaking sound decisions making for good results.

GM17076: UNDERSTANDING AND MANAGING
ORGANIZATIONAL BEHAVIOR. 

GM17076: 

From the very first day organization is established, it begins like humans to acquire behaviors and the characters of the operators and the environment it is located. As the organization grows, develops, matures and get more exposed to the admixture of internal and external influences, its behaviors assume more complex nature. This gets more complicated with mixed grills of culture and traditions when such advancement in expansion involves merger, acquisition and partnership within or across borders.
The result is a more challenging organization to manage. To succeed with understanding organization’s behavior industry leaders and managers must identify behavioral origins, its complexity, the pluses and minuses, how they play out and their dynamics. This course is essentially to build participants’ capacity to identify, understand and manage behaviors of their organizations for better performance. 

GM17077: FUNDS
PROCUREMENT AND
MANAGEMENT 

GM17077:

Fund is the main medium of exchange for other resources; works, goods and services and dominant measures for organization performance. In most cases, the terms and conditions for its acquisition elicit intense scrutiny and attention. Such exercises usually results in avoidable delays and associated inefficiencies.
This course is designed and delivered in hands-on experiential approach that raises participants’ capabilities and capabilities to successfully manage funds procurement. 

GM17078: GOAL SETTING, PLANNING AND
IMPLEMENTATION 

GM17078: 

Industry leaders and managers are periodically involved in setting goals for their organizations. Goals are expected to define a destination in a given time whether it is a vision, mission or operational objectives. As a destination, it has to be very clear in specific terms, so that when leaders and mangers arrive there, they know. It should also be possible to measure achievement of the goals as efforts are being exerted. The target must be encouraging and facilitates its achievement over a space of time. In most cases, putting goals together reveal some tendency to be covertly ambitious, allowing mere dreams to blur focus. The result of such is wasteful applications of resources. An attempt to plan for such goals is equally unrealistic and implementation misses the essence.
This course recognizes the importance of good goal setting, planning and implementation in organizations and responds with this course that is well crafted and offered to participants to fortify their knowledge, skills and expertise required to set, plan and implement organizational goals deploying today’s best practices and techniques. 

GM17079: NEGOTIATION
MANAGEMENT 

GM17079:

Organizations are always engaged in finding a comfortable position in every dealing with parties in the course of doing their businesses. The underlining principle in the case of cherished long relationship is that in finding a comfortable position, zero-sum game is avoided. All parties must win and remain in the relationship. This makes the negation management complex requiring special knowledge, skills and
expertise. This course enhances
participants’ ability to always identify
and understand terms, conditions andthe bases for undertaking negotiation
and manage it better.
